Alphabet & Pronunciation

Das Alphabet — 26 letters + 4 special characters

The German alphabet has the same 26 letters as English, plus 4 special characters unique to German: ä, ö, ü (called Umlauts) and ß (Eszett/sharp S).

Ä ä "eh" — like in "bed" ähnlich (similar)
Ö ö "uh" — rounded lips öffnen (to open)
Ü ü "ee" — rounded lips über (over/above)
ß "ss" — sharp S Straße (street)

02 Grammatik — Grammar
Grammar

Articles — der / die / das

Every German noun has a grammatical gender

der Masculine
der Mann (man) der Hund (dog) der Tisch (table) der Vater (father)

Common endings: -er, -en, -el, -ling, -ig, -ismus

die Feminine
die Frau (woman) die Katze (cat) die Schule (school) die Mutter (mother)

Common endings: -ung, -heit, -keit, -schaft, -tion, -tät, -e

das Neuter
das Kind (child) das Buch (book) das Auto (car) das Haus (house)

Common endings: -chen, -lein, -tum, -um, -ment, -nis

⚠️

The article changes in the plural — all genders use "die" for plural: die Männer, die Frauen, die Kinder

Definite vs Indefinite Articles

Grammar

Cases — Die Fälle

German has 4 grammatical cases that affect article endings

Nominativ Subject — who does the action Der Mann kauft Brot. (The man buys bread.)
Akkusativ Direct object — what receives the action Der Mann kauft den Hund. (The man buys the dog.)
Dativ Indirect object — to whom / for whom Ich gebe dem Mann das Buch. (I give the man the book.)
Genitiv Possession — of whom / whose Das Buch des Mannes. (The man's book.)

Verb Conjugation — Konjugation

Present tense (Präsens) regular & irregular verbs

German verbs change their ending based on the subject pronoun. Regular verbs follow a predictable pattern; irregular (strong) verbs have vowel changes in their stem.

Grammar

Modal Verbs — Modalverben

Modal verbs express ability, possibility, permission, or obligation. They are always paired with an infinitive verb at the end of the sentence.

können can / to be able to Ich kann Deutsch sprechen.
müssen must / to have to Du musst lernen.
wollen want to Er will schlafen.
sollen should / supposed to Wir sollen pünktlich sein.
dürfen may / allowed to Ihr dürft nicht rauchen.
mögen like / may Sie mögen Kaffee.
Grammar

Word Order — Satzstellung

German word order follows the V2 rule: the conjugated verb is always in the second position in a main clause, no matter what comes first.

Ich
lerne
Deutsch
heute.
I learn German today.
Heute
lerne
ich
Deutsch.
Today I learn German. (time moved to front)
⚠️

In subordinate clauses (with weil, dass, obwohl...), the verb moves to the end of the clause.
